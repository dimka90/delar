# DELAR: Problem and Solution Brief

## Project Summary

DELAR is a decentralized land registry and transfer platform designed to improve trust, traceability, and transparency in land records.

The current prototype is built around a Plateau State, Nigeria use case. It demonstrates how land records can be registered, reviewed, verified, listed, transferred, and historically tracked on-chain.

## The Problem DELAR Is Addressing

### 1. Unreliable Land Ownership Records

In Nigeria, land ownership is often difficult to prove with certainty because records may be incomplete, fragmented, paper-based, delayed, or hard to validate across institutions.

This creates problems such as:
- multiple claims on the same land
- forged or disputed land documents
- difficulty confirming who the real current owner is
- poor visibility into previous ownership

### 2. Opaque Property Transfer Process

Land transfers are often hard to track after the initial transaction.

This leads to:
- poor transfer traceability
- weak buyer confidence
- increased fraud risk
- difficulty auditing who transferred land to whom, when, and at what value

### 3. Slow and Inefficient Registration and Titling Workflow

Nigeria still faces major land administration challenges. In March 2025, the Federal Ministry of Housing and Urban Development stated that less than 10% of land in Nigeria is formally registered, and identified inefficient registration, weak ownership identification, and inadequate land information systems as key constraints.

For users, this means:
- long processing cycles
- weak documentation coverage
- high friction in proving ownership
- limited ability to unlock land as a trustworthy asset

### 4. Plateau State-Specific Opportunity

Plateau State already has an official digital land administration direction through PLAGIS, the Plateau Geographic Information Service.

PLAGIS states that it was created to:
- computerize land administration activities
- improve secure and speedy processing of land titles
- support transparent and seamless registry and cadastral workflows

This means the local problem is real and recognized institutionally:
- land administration needs stronger digital systems
- registry and cadastral records need better structure and accessibility
- title processing should be more secure, transparent, and efficient

## What DELAR Solves

DELAR is built to solve the trust and traceability gap in land records and transfers.

### Core Solution

DELAR provides:
- on-chain land record registration
- ownership-linked digital records
- verification workflow support
- listing and transfer workflow
- historical ownership trail for each title reference

### In Practical Terms

DELAR helps solve the following:

#### A. Ownership Traceability

DELAR records:
- who first registered a property
- who currently owns it
- who previously owned it
- when ownership changed
- the value of recorded transfers

This is stronger than a basic marketplace because it creates an auditable record trail.

#### B. Reduced Disputes and Better Trust

By maintaining a structured history of:
- registration
- verification
- listing
- transfer

DELAR reduces ambiguity around ownership state and transaction sequence.

#### C. Better Verification Framework

DELAR is designed to support an institutional verification model where submitted land credentials can be reviewed by Land and Survey or another authorized body before a record is treated as trusted and sale-ready.

This is important because:
- blockchain preserves records well
- but institutional verification is still needed to establish legal credibility of the original land data

So the long-term value proposition is:
- institutional review for authenticity
- blockchain for preservation, transparency, and transfer history

#### D. Stronger Transparency for Buyers

A buyer should be able to inspect:
- title reference
- property location
- verification status
- current owner
- previous owner
- transfer history

That directly improves buyer confidence and due diligence.

## The Specific Nigeria + Plateau State Framing

### Nigeria-Level Framing

DELAR addresses a national problem of weak land registration coverage, poor land information structure, and low trust in ownership documentation.

### Plateau State Framing

DELAR is especially relevant in Plateau State because the state already recognizes the need for:
- digital land administration
- faster title processing
- registry modernization
- better land information systems

DELAR can be presented as a blockchain-based registry layer that complements this direction by adding:
- tamper-resistant ownership records
- verifiable transfer history
- transparent marketplace workflow for verified land

## What DELAR Is Today

The current prototype demonstrates:
- land registration
- record storage
- on-chain activity history
- verification workflow logic
- listing flow
- purchase flow
- ownership history display

## What DELAR Is Designed to Become

DELAR is designed to evolve into a platform that works alongside institutional actors such as Land and Survey.

The intended future workflow is:
1. User submits land credentials
2. Land and Survey verifies the submission
3. The record is marked verified
4. Ownership and transfer history are preserved on-chain
5. Buyers can inspect verified history before purchase

## Presentation-Ready Problem Statement

DELAR solves the problem of unreliable land records and opaque property transfers by creating a transparent on-chain system for land registration, verification workflow, ownership tracking, and transfer history.

## Presentation-Ready One-Line Pitch

DELAR is a decentralized land registry and transfer platform built for Plateau State, Nigeria, to improve trust, traceability, and transparency in land ownership records and property transactions.
